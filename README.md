# CS 255: System Analysis and Design

# Portfolio Reflection

## Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The DriverPass project focused on designing a system for DriverPass, a company that helps students prepare for their DMV driving exams. The client wanted a cloud-based web application where customers could register, purchase training packages, schedule driving lessons, take online practice tests, and manage their accounts. The system also included features for employees, the owner, and the IT administrator to manage users, appointments, reports, and DMV updates.

## What did you do particularly well?

I think I did a good job translating the client’s requirements into a complete system design. My UML diagrams, business requirements, and technical requirements all supported one another and reflected the features the client requested. I also made sure to consider the different user roles and how each person would interact with the system.

## If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I could revise one part of the project, I would improve the UML diagrams by adding more detail and making the relationships between different components even clearer. I have a much better understanding of UML diagrams now, and I would organize them in a way that is easier for developers to follow.

## How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I started by identifying what the client wanted the system to accomplish and then converted those requirements into system features, functional requirements, and UML diagrams. Every design decision was based on solving the client’s problem. Considering the user’s needs is important because the system has to be practical, easy to use, and support the tasks people actually need to complete.

## How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I approach software design by first understanding the problem before thinking about the technical solution. I like breaking the system into smaller pieces, identifying the different users, and mapping out how they interact with the system using UML diagrams. In future projects, I would spend more time gathering requirements early, creating prototypes, and getting user feedback before finalizing the design.
